Tombs of Terror Behind the Scenes

17/09/2019

A few days ago we had the opportunity to sit down and ask Hearthstone Game designer Alec Dawson and Lead mission designer Dave Kosak some questions regarding the Tombs of Terror PvE adventure that just launched. While we haven’t yet had the chance to play the adventure ourselves, and so our guides will be published as we progress through the content in the upcoming days, we wanted to share some of the more interesting design decisions and behind-the-scenes information about the new adventure to really pique your interest and get you hyped for some boss fighting.

A major new feature in this adventure is the inclusion of dual-class heroes for the player. Each of the four explorers is a blend of two Hearthstone classes, chosen to balance out synergies and combo potential with fidelity to the identity of the characters. All classes except Warlock are represented and we were told that this is due to the fact that Rafaam himself is a Warlock and that no member of the League of Explorers has any sort of association with Warlock flavor or lore.

The interesting thing about Tombs is that the player will be able to unlock “signature treasures” for each hero. These treasures persevere and keep their upgrades from run to run, giving a lasting advantage to the player who takes the time to level them up. Treasures, paired with all-new bosses that have absurdly large health-pools which also persevere through runs, give the adventure a much more progression-based feel than previous boss-run adventures like the Kobolds’ Dungeon Run or even Witchwood’s Monster Hunt.

The final boss brings all the equipment that the player has collected to that point together in one huge showdown. Collecting and upgrading equipment gives the adventure a sense of progression, and paired with large boss health pools, was compared to World of Warcraft by some of the attendees. One thing is sure, this system will be more satisfying for new or less experienced players who like to get a sense of powering up and chipping away at the boss’s health progressively but it also provides a tougher challenge to players who want to try to one-shot each boss through crazy combos and flawless play without relying on treasures too much.

This brings us to what seems to be the name of the game when it comes to Blizzard’s current adventure design - giving players options and keeping adventures accessible and fun for everyone. Alec highlighted that the player experience is a major factor even when tweaking the boss AI - making sure it does not completely brick (like the notorious Kel’Thuzad strategies on Lich King) but also ensuring that it does not delve into territory that feels like cheating to the players and negatively impacts their fun.

When it comes to the story of Saviours of Uldum, Dave mentioned that a year-long narrative revolving around the League of E.V.I.L. that spans several expansions is something the team had a lot of fun writing for fans of Hearthstone lore. However, they also want to ensure that each expansion is captivating and stands out as an individual chapter in the game’s life; so that following the overarching story isn’t necessary to appreciate an adventure on its own.

In any case, we are excited to delve into the Tombs of Terror and will be posting content from the YaLLa team in the upcoming days so stay tuned!

Final Facts

Don’t hold your breath tier:
  • Hearthstone Battle Pass

    • The team needs to assess what people expect from a battle pass and will have to juggle expectations from players. Nothing to announce yet.

  • Tournament Mode

    • Work was done, might be revisited.

  • Adventure-related Tavern Brawl reruns

    • No plans to rerun these brawls as of now but they might appear in an end-of-year recap.

  • 2v2 modes

    • These would pose a UI challenge but nothing unsolvable. The team talks about it a lot and would love to implement it in the future. Nothing to announce as of now.

  • More hardcore challenges for players

    • The philosophy is to make content that works for everybody and not gate anything behind challenges that are too difficult.

  • Monks and demon hunters

    • The team wants to focus on the class identity of the classes they do have as of now rather than creating new classes or hero cards.

  • Custom boards
  • Hearthstone on Switch
  • Cosmetics to distinguish veterans past just golden heroes
Maybe tier
  • Adjusting the new player experience

    • The new player experience is due for a revamp but nothing major - they want to keep it short and sweet.

  • More highlander support

    • The team did not want to reveal anything official for upcoming expansions but did not deny the addition of more highlander cards. They discussed that highlander decks are automatically affected by a new set regardless of whether they have direct support or not.

  • Classic and basic set rotation/adjustments

    • The team wants to ensure these sets support a class’s identity and sets them up for success but rotation is “on their radar.”
